Nominal Surge, Real Stagnation, and the Affordability Crisis 2010-2024

NoteKey Takeaways
  • Nominal surge, real stagnation. KFE rose ~4,800% nominally (2010-2024); in real terms the index peaked at 153.7 in 2022 and fell to 118.4 by 2024.
  • Affordability collapsed. Months of minimum wage to buy 100 m2 rose from ~110 in 2010 to ~276 in 2022 – a 2.5x worsening.
  • Exchange rate pass-through is strong. Quarterly USD/TRY and KFE changes are strongly correlated; TRY depreciation episodes directly precede KFE spikes.
  • Istanbul and Aegean lead, Southeast lags. Five-year cumulative nominal gains (2020-2024) range from ~2,100% to ~1,400% across 26 NUTS-2 regions.
  • Supply is contracting. Building permits fell from 1.22 million units in 2015 to 549,000 in 2024 – a 55% drop.

1.1 The two stories of Turkish housing prices

  scale_y_continuous(labels = comma) +
  labs(
    title    = "Nominal vs. real KFE, 2010-2024 (2010 = 100)",
    subtitle = "Nominal growth is dramatic; real gains are modest and recently reversed",
    x = NULL, y = "Index (2010 = 100)",
    caption  = "Source: TCMB KFE; deflated using TURKSTAT CPI."
  ) +
  theme(axis.text.x = element_text(angle = 45, hjust = 1))

The nominal index rose ~48x from 2010 to 2024. The real index peaked at 153.7 in 2022 – just 54% above its 2010 level – before declining. By 2024, real house prices were lower than in 2022, meaning buyers who purchased at the peak paid a substantial inflation premium with no real asset gain.

Conclusions

Four structural findings emerge from this analysis:

  1. The Turkish housing boom is largely nominal, not real. KFE rose ~48x nominally but the real index peaked in 2022 and has since declined. Buyers in 2021-2023 effectively paid an inflation premium.

  2. Affordability has collapsed to crisis levels. The minimum-wage-months metric worsened 2.5x by 2022 and continues to deteriorate, reflecting a decade of supply under-investment plus income growth unable to keep pace with price inflation or currency depreciation.

  3. Exchange rate risk is the dominant driver. USD/TRY depreciation episodes directly precede or coincide with the sharpest nominal KFE increases. Turkish housing has acted as a partial inflation hedge in lira terms, drawing domestic savings away from financial assets.

  4. The supply pipeline signals prolonged undersupply. The 55% fall in building permits since 2015, combined with post-earthquake relocation demand and collapsed mortgage activity, means structural supply constraints will persist even if nominal price growth slows further.

### Key Takeaways

## Conclusions

Four structural findings emerge from this analysis:

1. **The Turkish housing boom is largely nominal, not real.** KFE rose ~48x
   nominally but the real index peaked in 2022 and has since declined.
   Buyers in 2021-2023 effectively paid an inflation premium.

2. **Affordability has collapsed to crisis levels.** The minimum-wage-months
   metric worsened 2.5x by 2022 and continues to deteriorate, reflecting a
   decade of supply under-investment plus income growth unable to keep pace
   with price inflation or currency depreciation.

3. **Exchange rate risk is the dominant driver.** USD/TRY depreciation
   episodes directly precede or coincide with the sharpest nominal KFE
   increases. Turkish housing has acted as a partial inflation hedge in lira
   terms, drawing domestic savings away from financial assets.

4. **The supply pipeline signals prolonged undersupply.** The 55% fall in
   building permits since 2015, combined with post-earthquake relocation
   demand and collapsed mortgage activity, means structural supply constraints
   will persist even if nominal price growth slows further.
